# EXP_002

## Goal
To get a baseline result on UCDDB (St. Vincent's University Hospital / University College Dublin Sleep Apnea Database) using a 1D CNN model for binary sleep apnea detection. Target metric is Macro F1 > 0.50 on held-out test patients.


## Setup
- **Dataset**: UCDDB (25 patient recordings in total).
- **Patient-Level Split**: 80/20 patient-level train/test split.
  - Train patients (20): `ucddb002` through `ucddb023`.
  - Test patients (5): `ucddb024` through `ucddb028`.
- **Signal Processing**:
  - Resampled EDF signals to 10 Hz (`TARGET_SFREQ = 10.0`).
  - Windowing: 30-second non-overlapping windows (300 samples per window).
  - Channel Count: 14 channels (channel guard filter applied).
- **Labeling Rule**: Binary classification (0 = Normal, 1 = Apnea/Hypopnea). A window is labeled as Apnea (`1`) if any apnea or hypopnea event overlaps the 30-second window by at least 10 seconds (`OVERLAP_THRESHOLD_SECS = 10`).
- **Normalization**: Per-subject z-score normalization computed using normal windows (`label=0`) only, clipped to $[-5.0, +5.0]$ standard deviations to suppress motion artifacts.
- **Model Architecture**: `ApneaCNN` (1D CNN with 3 convolutional blocks: Conv1D(14→64, k=7), Conv1D(64→128, k=5), Conv1D(128→256, k=3), followed by Global Average Pooling, Dropout(0.3), and Linear classifier layers).
- **Training Parameters**:
  - Optimizer: Adam (learning rate = $1\times 10^{-3}$)
  - Loss Function: `BCEWithLogitsLoss` with positive class weight `pos_weight = 5.0` (capped at 5.0x to mitigate class imbalance).
  - Batch Size: 64
  - Epochs: 20

## Observations
- **High Overall Accuracy is Misleading**: Overall accuracy is 70.68%, but this is heavily inflated by the majority class (True Normal count TN = 2,787 out of 3,351 normal test windows).
- **Severe Under-Detection of Apnea Events**: At the default decision threshold ($t = 0.50$), the model detected only 91 out of 721 actual apnea epochs, yielding an extremely poor **Sensitivity (Recall) of 12.6%** and **Precision of 13.9%**.
- **Dominance of Errors**: Both False Positives ($\text{FP} = 564$) and False Negatives ($\text{FN} = 630$) significantly outnumber True Positives ($\text{TP} = 91$).
- **Low Precision Across Threshold Sweep**: Sweeping the classification threshold from $t = 0.05$ to $t = 0.85$ shows that lowering the threshold to $0.05$ improves sensitivity to 84.2%, but precision remains constrained between 14.0% and 17.7%.
- **Macro F1 Performance Ceiling**: Macro F1 peaks at **0.478** at threshold $t = 0.50$, failing to reach the target benchmark of $> 0.50$.


## Hypothesis
1. **Model Capacity & Optimization Deficit (Model Not Learning)**: The current 1D CNN architecture and loss formulation fail to learn generalized discriminative feature representations even though the training loss goes down. The model rapidly plateaus during training and collapses toward predicting the majority (normal) class to minimize loss, indicating optimization stalls or insufficient representation depth for complex bio-signals.
2. **High Inter-Subject Variability & Limited Cohort Size**: With only 25 total subjects (20 train, 5 test), individual physiological differences (e.g., baseline respiration amplitude, heart rate variability, anatomical variations) create significant subject-level distribution shifts. The model overfits to training subject baselines and fails when evaluated on unseen patients.
3. **Non-Stratified Train/Test Patient Split**: Splitting patients sequentially (`ucddb002`–`ucddb023` vs `ucddb024`–`ucddb028`) without stratifying by Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI) or event distribution results in unbalanced evaluation. Test subjects may possess vastly different event severities, noise levels, or baseline characteristics than the training cohort.
4. **Excessive Downsampling & Indiscriminate Channel Pooling**: Uniformly downsampling all channels to 10 Hz discards high-frequency signal components (such as EEG spindles/beta waves or sharp respiratory variations) critical for detecting subtle hypopnea events. Furthermore, feeding all 14 channels into the network indiscriminately introduces uncalibrated or noisy channels, degrading signal-to-noise ratio and confusing feature representations.
5. **Noisey Channel**: In total 14 channels are used, not all channels are important for detection. It could be possible that some channels are noisy and degrades performance


## Interpretation and Conclusion
- **Conclusion**: The baseline `ApneaCNN` model **failed** to achieve the target performance metric on UCDDB, achieving a Macro F1 score of **0.4779** (below the target threshold of $> 0.50$).
- **Key Takeaway**: A localized, single-window 1D CNN without temporal context, stratification, or channel-selective preprocessing is insufficient for robust sleep apnea detection across unseen patients.
- **Recommended Action Plan**:
  1. **Incorporate Sequence / Context Modeling**: Implement recurrent architectures (LSTM/GRU) or temporal convolutional networks (TCN / Transformer) across adjacent 30-second windows to capture multi-epoch dynamics.
  2. **Implement Stratified Patient Splitting**: Stratify train/test splits based on subject AHI and event rates to ensure balanced dataset distributions.
  3. **Refine Channel Selection & Sampling**: Preserve higher sampling rates for key respiratory/EEG channels and filter out uninformative/noisy channels.
  4. **Loss & Architectural Enhancements**: Replace Global Average Pooling with temporal attention and experiment with Focal Loss or re-weighted BCE loss to prevent majority class collapse.
